# Competitor Research

## Goal

Provide useful external context and competitive teardown without fabricating sources, scores, or claims.

## Workflow

1. Define the product decision the research must inform and the required recency window.
2. Identify 2-4 direct or adjacent competitors from context and state why each belongs in the comparison.
3. Choose comparison dimensions before researching: target user, job, workflow, pricing, positioning, feature depth, trust/safety, onboarding, integrations, GTM, analytics, support, mobile fit, accessibility, or operational model.
4. Use available search or browsing tools and record the research date.
5. Prefer primary sources for factual claims: official product pages, docs, pricing pages, help centers, release notes, app store listings, filings, or public API docs.
6. Use secondary sources only for market framing, commentary, or claims that cannot be verified from primary sources.
7. Capture source title, URL, publisher, publication or access date, observed fact, confidence, and whether the fact is inferred.
8. For flow teardown, capture entry point, required inputs, happy path, fallback path, permission or plan boundary, copy tone, and platform differences.
9. Score only dimensions with evidence. Do not create numeric scores for unsupported dimensions.
10. Separate observed facts from inference, product implications, and recommendations.
11. Mark unavailable, paywalled, stale, contradictory, or geographically limited information instead of filling gaps.
12. Convert findings into concrete product implications: defend, adapt, avoid, investigate, ignore, quick win, medium-term bet, or strategic watchlist.

## Boundary

This is the only PM Copilot skill for competitor research, competitive teardown, pricing comparison, positioning analysis, and battlecard inputs. Do not create a sibling teardown skill; extend this skill when a new competitor-analysis method is absorbed.

## Output

- Research question
- Research method and date
- Evidence table with source, date, fact, confidence, and limitation
- Competitor comparison table using decision-relevant dimensions
- Flow teardown table when onboarding, UX, pricing, or plan boundaries matter
- Evidence-backed scorecard only for sourced dimensions
- SWOT, positioning, or battlecard notes when useful
- Product implications
- Quick wins, medium-term bets, strategic watchlist, limitations, and unknowns

## Quality Bar

- Source-backed claims include references.
- Unavailable information is marked unavailable.
- Implications are tied to the current product task.
- No claim relies on memory when browsing or provided source evidence is required.
- Recommendations distinguish fact, inference, and opinion.
- Scores do not appear without supporting evidence.
- Recommendations fit current product constraints and do not chase parity for its own sake.
- Pricing, availability, policy, and model/version claims include a current source or are marked as unverified.
